Case Study: Designing a Referral Flow for an Ed-Tech Platform

Context

Sensei, an ed-tech platform, wanted to introduce a referral program to increase sign-ups among parents and influencers. The goal was to make the referral process simple, transparent, and rewarding, while aligning with the platform's mission of helping children build emotional and academic growth.

Role:

UI/UX Designer (sole designer for this feature)

Timeline:

2 Weeks

Tools:

Figma, Illustrator, After Effects

Problem Statement

Sensei, our ed-tech platform, aimed to grow its user base by encouraging parents and influencers to invite others to join. While the idea of a referral program was clear, the challenge was how to design a referral flow that:

  1. Motivated users with clear, tangible rewards.
  2. Worked seamlessly across mobile and desktop.
  3. Balanced two different user types.
  4. Reduced friction in the referral process.

The team had noticed that previous referral attempts on other ed-tech apps often failed because:

  • Rewards were unclear or delayed.
  • Users couldn't track whether their referral was successful.
  • The process felt complicated or hidden.

The design challenge:

How might we create a referral experience that feels simple, transparent, and rewarding, while aligning with Sensei's mission of building trust with parents and influencers?

Research

1. User Personas

Parent Persona

  • • Profile: 35-year-old mother, working professional, has a 7-year-old child.
  • • Motivation: Wants quality education and emotional learning for her child at affordable costs.
  • • Goals:
    • Access engaging and trustworthy learning platforms.
    • Save money while ensuring quality.
  • • Pain Points:
    • Confused by complicated referral processes.
    • Doesn't trust vague reward systems.

Influencer Persona

  • • Profile: 28-year-old content creator, 50k Instagram followers.
  • • Motivation: Recommend credible platforms & build reputation.
  • • Goals:
    • Earn rewards or recognition.
    • Easily share referral codes/links.
  • • Pain Points:
    • Existing apps don't provide transparent tracking.
    • Rewards often feel too small or irrelevant.

Competitive Analysis

We studied referral programs of apps like Duolingo, Byju's, and Khan Academy.

  • • Strengths Found:
    • Duolingo: Clear progress tracking.
    • Byju's: Monetary discounts.
  • • Weaknesses Found:
    • Many hide referral under multiple clicks.
    • Rewards delayed.

Key Insights

  • • Transparency builds trust → users must see status.
  • • Immediate gratification → instant coupon boosts motivation.
  • • Ease of sharing → simple buttons are crucial.

Design Goals & Solution Approach

Design Goals

Based on research insights, we set these goals:

  1. Simplicity: 2-3 clicks max.
  2. Transparency: Clear status tracking.
  3. Motivation: Instant gratification.
  4. Flexibility: WhatsApp, Email, Copy Link.

Solution Approach

We designed the flow as follows:

  1. Entry Point: Prominent "Invite & Earn" CTA.
  2. Referral Generation: Unique link with one-tap sharing.
  3. Reward Feedback: Real-time updates on friend joining.
  4. Redemption: "My Rewards" section for coupons.

User Flow: Referral Reward Flow

We have two main user types: Parents and Influencers.

Step 1: Discover

• Parent sees CTA on dashboard.

Step 2: Share

• Tap to open share options.

Step 3: Friend Joins

• Friend clicks link and signs up.

Step 4: Reward

• Instant notification of reward.

Step 5: Redeem

• Coupon applied at checkout.

End of Case Study

Case Study: Wall Fabrica – Interior Cost Calculator

1. Problem

When people plan home interiors, they get confused about pricing.

  • No clear idea of total cost
  • Too many hidden charges
  • Hard to compare materials and options
  • Customers hesitate to take the next step

👉 Result: Users drop off before contacting the company.

2. Goal

Design a simple and transparent calculator that:

  • Shows instant cost estimation
  • Helps users customize their choices
  • Builds trust with clear pricing
  • Encourages users to book a consultation

3. My Approach

Step 1: Understand the User

Target users:

  • Homeowners
  • First-time interior buyers
  • People with budget confusion

User mindset:

“Kitna kharcha hoga? Hidden cost toh nahi hai?”

Step 2: Simplify the Experience

I broke the calculator into easy steps:

  1. Select space (Bedroom, Living Room, etc.)
  2. Choose materials / finishes
  3. Adjust size or quantity
  4. See instant price update

👉 No complex forms. Only guided flow.

4. Design Decisions

1. Step-by-step flow

• Avoid overwhelming screens
• One decision at a time

2. Real-time price update

• Price changes instantly when user selects options
• Builds trust

3. Highlight total cost

• Final cost always visible
• Sticky CTA at bottom

4. Color usage

• Orange = action (CTA, highlights)
• White = clean and premium feel
• Light grey = background for clarity

5. Key Features

✅ Instant price calculation ✅ Easy customization ✅ Clear breakdown of cost ✅ Mobile-friendly design ✅ Strong CTA

6. Challenges & Solutions

1. Too many options

Interior has many combinations.

👉 Solution: Grouped options into simple categories

2. Trust issue

Users don’t trust pricing tools.

👉 Solution: Transparent breakdown, No hidden values, Clean UI

3. Drop-offs

Users leave if flow is long.

👉 Solution: Reduced steps, Sticky button, Quick interactions

7. Result (Expected Impact)

  • 📈 Increased user engagement
  • 📈 Higher inquiry / booking rate
  • 📉 Reduced confusion
  • 💡 Better brand trust

8. What I Learned

1. Simplicity is more powerful than features

2. Pricing clarity = trust

3. Step-by-step UX works best for complex decisions

End of Case Study
